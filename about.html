Why is this happening?

The Pennsylvania Constitution requires the governor and the General Assembly to pass a budget for the state by midnight, June 30, the last day of the state's fiscal year. In June 2015 they failed to do that. On December 29, Governer Wolf signed a "garbage" partial budget delivered to him by the Republican-led State Assembly, line-item vetoing more than half of it.

At odds are issues of education funding and balancing spending with taxation.

Now, months later, though some funding has been freed up, the impasse remains with no end in sight.

What is this website?

Pennsylvania Budget Clock aggregates stories of the impact of the budget impasse in order to impose a greater sense of urgency and pressure Pennsylvania politicians to act.
