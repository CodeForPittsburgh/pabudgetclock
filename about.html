Why is this happening?

Each year, the governor and the Illinois General Assembly are constitutionally required to pass a budget for the state. Last year, they failed to do that, and we still don’t have a budget.

Leading up to the budget deadline on July 1st, 2015, both Republican Gov. Rauner and the Democratically-controlled legislature, led by House Speaker Madigan and Senate President Cullerton, presented their own wildly different budget proposals.

Gov. Bruce Rauner
House Speaker Madigan
Senate President Cullerton

The debate is over Gov. Rauner’s Turnaround Agenda which, among other things, proposes stripping collective bargaining rights from unions, which Democrats oppose. As a result, neither side has come to an agreement over the state budget, and both continue to blame each other for the deadlock.

Now, months later, the impasse remains with no end in sight.

What is this website?

Illinois Budget Clock aggregates stories of the impact of the budget impasse in order to impose a greater sense of urgency and pressure Illinois politicians to act.

To stay posted, follow ILBudgetClock on Twitter and Facebook.
